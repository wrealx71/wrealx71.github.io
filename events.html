ABOUT WREAL X

“I don’t just make music. I bleed into the mic.”

In the heart of East Africa, where the Nile begins its ancient journey and the red soil of Yei town tells stories of resilience, a new voice emerged. Not with a shout, but with a melody—part blues, part bravado, and completely unfiltered.

That voice belongs to Angel Jambu Roy, known to the world as Wreal X.

Some call him "the African Juice WRLD." But Wreal X is not a copy. He is a testament to what happens when raw South Sudanese emotion meets the global language of Emo trap and Afrobeats. He is a Singer, Emo Rapper, and Songwriter—a sonic architect who builds bridges between the dusty streets of Yei and the neon-lit landscapes of Chicago and London.

📍 The Genesis: Yei Town, 2007

Born on the 20th of July, 2007, in Yei Town, Equatoria, South Sudan, Wreal X's story begins with both love and loss. The son of the late Mr. Taban Roy and Mrs. Santuke Koloy Kamba, Angel learned early that life is a mixture of joy and grief—a duality that would later define his music.

At just ten years old, while other children were playing, he was writing. Hip-hop found him first—the rhythm, the poetry, the unapologetic truth-telling. But it wasn't until he discovered Emo rap that he found his home.

"I heard Juice WRLD say 'I'll be fine, just not today' and I felt like he was speaking my diary."

🎵 The Sound: Where Afrobeats Meets a Broken Heart

Wreal X is not a one-genre artist. He moves with ease between:

  • Emo Trap – for the 2 AM thoughts, the anxiety, the romantic scars.
  • Afrobeats – for the rhythm of the motherland, the joy beneath the pain.
  • Rap verses – for the life realities: poverty, ambition, loss, and the struggle to dream.

But through every beat change, one thing remains constant: honesty.

🌟 The Inspirations

Wreal X stands on the shoulders of emotional rebels: Juice WRLD, XXXTentacion, Lil Uzi Vert, and Trippie Redd. But unlike his idols, Wreal X carries an African heartbeat. When he raps about heartbreak, you hear the ache of a displaced generation. When he sings Afrobeats, you hear the hope of a nation rebuilding.

❌ Why "Wreal X"?

Because authenticity is rare. Angel Jambu Roy chose to be wreal—real with a twist. The "X" marks the unknown, the future, the variable that can't be predicted. He is not just an artist. He is a movement from the youngest country in the world.
